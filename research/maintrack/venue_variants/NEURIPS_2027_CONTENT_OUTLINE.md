# NeurIPS 2027 Scientific Content Outline

Status: outcome-blind outline; official 2027 format pending

Working title: **VERA: Evidence-Efficient Certification of Representation Edits
Under Deployment Shift**

The title changes to **Support-Aware Certification** if the frozen
evidence-efficient title-retention rule fails.

## Abstract

Lead with the statistical decision problem: certify or abstain on one of a
finite set of representation interventions when deployment may reweight
supported conditionals. State the vector confidence envelope, additive
multi-cell evidence allocation, exact-KL lower bound, and unsupported-support
impossibility. Attribute LTT and robust-risk machinery, then report the same IID
and controlled empirical statements as every other variant.

## 1. A Statistical Decision Layer for Representation Edits

Define the deployment contract and why average validation performance is
insufficient. State exactly four shared contributions. Emphasize the distinction
between inherited finite-family testing and the proposed intervention-specific
population object, evidence requirement, and support boundary.

## 2. Prior Risk Control, Robust Evaluation, and Allocation

Position LTT, CRC, Pareto Testing, Prompt Risk Control, robust validation,
CVaR concentration, distributional fairness certification, active testing,
stratified/Neyman allocation, fixed-budget feasibility testing, and shared-
observation experimental design. Incorporate every result of the final frozen
literature refresh, including collisions that narrow novelty.

## 3. Supported Conditional Reweighting Model

Define the probability space, sigma-field fixed before certification, paired
harm in `{-1,0,1}`, Bernoulli attacker correctness, balanced leakage, conditional
density-ratio ambiguity sets, vector profiles, environment mixtures, source
prevalence standardization, and missing cells. State identity and multiplicity
treatment explicitly.

## 4. Fixed-Profile and Simultaneous Certificates

Derive the CVaR identity, DKW robust-risk curve, exact discrete substitutions,
candidate-wise IUT, simultaneous envelope, continuum inversion, post-inspection
profile validity, and common radius. Explain why no uncountable union bound is
needed and why the fixed-profile error allocation differs from the envelope's
familywise coverage.

## 5. Evidence Requirements and Allocation

State the sufficient sample-size theorem, one-cell square-score allocation,
additive multi-cell power condition, convex minimax program, two-thirds-power
special case, one-cell reduction, exact-KL lower bound, compact-interior local
scaling, and inverse-budget planning diagnostic. Restrict optimality to the
registered separate-stream DKW surrogate and distinguish continuous from
integer claims.

## 6. Unsupported-Support Impossibility

Give the full two-world theorem and quantitative acceptance consequence. Cover
missing target environments and missing source classes. Use Camelyon17 only as
an identification-boundary instantiation and state which new support or
structural assumptions could make certification possible.

## 7. Controlled Experimental Design

Describe all datasets, official eraser entry points, candidates, attackers,
seeds, thresholds, streams, exact finite laws, density-ratio checks, evidence
budgets, allocations, nine rules, independent units, primary gates, and
registered secondary analyses. Explain why controlled replays establish
ambiguity membership but do not create new people or population extrapolation.

## 8. Safety, Power, and Geometry Results

Report the same primary table and figure as the other variants. Give NeurIPS
more space for theory/data overlays, abstention-versus-evidence curves,
false-acceptance bounds, exact small-case checks, vector/common geometry,
limiting-coordinate frequencies, and sensitivity to Gamma and budget. Preserve
all failed gates and zero-denominator cases.

## 9. Allocation and Lower-Bound Experiments

Compare uniform, locked square-score, and additive allocation on disjoint common
random streams. Report continuous objectives, integer gaps, safe retention,
safety events, inverse-budget top-ups, and per-dataset heterogeneity. Show where
the empirical result agrees or disagrees with the surrogate. A null allocation
result is a negative ablation and forces title narrowing.

## 10. Intervention and Attacker Stress Tests

Report the five eraser families as one frontier rather than a leaderboard, the
full attacker portfolio, held-out boosted-tree stress, and component ablations.
Keep GaitPDB as a detailed evidence diagnosis and the prior IID composite miss
as a precise historical result.

## 11. Limitations and Responsible Use

State finite-reference, registered-candidate, registered-attacker, supported-
law, declared-Gamma, threshold-design, separate-stream, algorithmic-seed, and
nonclinical limits. Include the final policy-verified AI disclosure and human
responsibility statement.

## NeurIPS Supplement

Include full proofs, exact-law derivations, all small-case checks, theorem/code
mapping, solver diagnostics, complete result tables, every ablation, receipt
canonicalization, upstream and license records, clean reproduction, and all
unfavorable analyses.
