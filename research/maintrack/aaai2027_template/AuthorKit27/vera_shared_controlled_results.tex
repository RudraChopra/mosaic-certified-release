\providecommand{\ControlledShiftAbstractResult}{In the prospectively registered controlled-shift study, VERA made 38 deployments with 0 shifted-contract violations, while validation selection violated 19/186 deployed decisions. The result is not an overall confirmatory success because the preregistered usefulness lower bound was 15.1\%, below the 20\% gate.}
\providecommand{\ControlledPrimaryResult}{The primary controlled-shift result is mixed, not a confirmatory pass: validation selection violated 19/186 deployed decisions, whereas the VERA vector envelope deployed 38 edits with 0 violations; the paired sign test favored VERA (18 favorable, 0 adverse, $p=7.6e-06$), but the preregistered usefulness gate failed because safe retention was 38/187 (20.3\%) with 95\% seed-bootstrap CI [15.1\%, 25.5\%], whose lower endpoint is below 20\%.}
\providecommand{\ControlledSafetyResult}{The registered rotating-sentinel safety gate passed with 0/64 false acceptances and a one-sided 95\% Clopper--Pearson upper bound of 4.6\%. Across all primary VERA vector deployments, shifted-law violations were 0/38.}
\providecommand{\ControlledRetentionResult}{Safe retention was 38/187 (20.3\%), below the registered lower-bound requirement, while the common-radius rule retained 6/187 (3.2\%); the vector/common ratio was 6.33 with 95\% interval [3.5, 18.5], so the vector advantage gate passed even though usefulness did not.}
\providecommand{\ControlledAllocationResult}{The preregistered secondary evidence-size sweep shows higher retention at larger budgets: at $\Gamma=1.1$ and budget 8,000, the vector rule retained 49/187 targeted-allocation opportunities and 51/187 uniform-allocation opportunities with zero deployed violations. These secondary rows do not replace the failed primary gate.}
\providecommand{\ControlledEvidenceResult}{The primary geometry shows a narrow supported envelope: among 38 vector deployments, the median common radius was 1.030, with range [1.001, 1.182]. The most frequent limiting coordinate was target::environment=0 (27 of 38 deployments), followed by source::0.}
\providecommand{\ControlledGaitResult}{GaitPDB was not an all-abstain failure: the vector rule deployed 8/64 seeds and retained 8/29 oracle-safe opportunities with zero deployed violations, but its result remains secondary and cannot rescue the primary usefulness miss.}
\providecommand{\ControlledHeldoutResult}{The held-out boosted-tree stress is outside the formal registered attacker guarantee: it found 2 violations among 38 vector deployments (5.3\% unsafe), so the paper reports it as portfolio-scope stress rather than as a coverage claim.}
\providecommand{\ControlledNegativeResults}{The failed usefulness gate is mandatory negative evidence. The threshold-stress headline is therefore ineligible despite severe naive failures at $\kappa=1$ (always deploy max 67.2\%, validation selection max 25.0\%) and VERA's measured 0.0\% violation rate in every stress cell. No secondary budget, held-out attacker, or dataset-specific result is promoted to a primary success.}
