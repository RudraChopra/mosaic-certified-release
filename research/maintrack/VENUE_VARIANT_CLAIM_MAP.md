# Three-Venue Claim Map

This outcome-blind map binds the AAAI-27, ICLR 2027, and NeurIPS 2027 content
variants to one claim set. Different exposition is allowed; different science
is not. Section locations are working-source locations until official ICLR and
NeurIPS 2027 formats are available.

## Shared Negative Results and Boundaries

All variants must retain:

1. every failed controlled primary gate and the overall IUT result;
2. all nine deployment rules, including rules that never deploy;
3. all supported datasets and every unfavorable per-dataset result;
4. all five eraser families without proxy or leaderboard claims;
5. every registered budget, shift cap, allocation, ablation, and attacker
   portfolio;
6. zero-denominator and right-censoring reason codes;
7. a failed three-rule threshold stress under the exact shared negative-result
   identifier, with no supplementary-severity headline rescue;
8. the prior Gait baseline-severity miss;
9. Camelyon17 as an unsupported-support boundary rather than an efficacy row;
10. finite-reference, support, portfolio, target-learner, separate-stream,
   threshold-design, algorithmic-seed, and nonclinical limitations; and
11. the same full-scope AI-assistance and human-responsibility disclosure,
    subject to final verified venue policy.

## Title and Venue Boundaries

`Evidence-Efficient` remains in all variants only when the shared manifest's
frozen literature and allocation conditions both pass. Otherwise all variants
use the support-aware fallback title. A venue may not keep the stronger title
because its reviewers see a different paper.

AAAI has an available official format. ICLR and NeurIPS content remains format
pending until official 2027 materials are verified. No content-complete label
authorizes simultaneous archival review or overrides current venue policy.

## Pass Condition

The map passes only when a final audit verifies every row against all three
flattened sources and PDFs, the shared result manifest, the authoritative
theory, bibliography, claim ledger, negative-result coverage, and title branch.
A human verifier must resolve any semantic mismatch that a text comparison
cannot decide.
