# VERA Claim Ledger

## Purpose

This ledger is the submission-facing contract between the manuscript and the
claim-grade evidence. Machine audits verify consistency and provenance; they do
not prove novelty, theorem correctness, ethical eligibility, or acceptance.

## Allowed Claims

### Method Claims

VERA may claim to audit a finite, preregistered family of representation edits
and either select an edit satisfying paired target-harm and balanced-leakage
contracts at a declared shift profile or abstain. The finite-candidate
acceptance mechanism must be attributed to Learn Then Test and related risk
control, including Prompt Risk Control; it is not a novelty claim.

VERA may claim a simultaneous support-aware representation-edit shift envelope. Target
coordinates bound within-environment density ratios for paired incremental
harm. Leakage coordinates bound within-source-class density ratios for every
registered retrained attacker, with the two class recalls averaged equally.
The common-radius summary is the diagonal of this vector envelope. The
certificate applies only to deployment laws satisfying the stated support and
conditional density-ratio assumptions.

VERA may claim that arbitrary observed-environment mixtures preserve a target
threshold once every environment-conditional contract is controlled, and that
balanced leakage is invariant to source prevalence. It must not claim that
every independently specified collection of environment/source conditionals is
jointly realizable.

VERA may claim a two-world impossibility boundary: if a required deployment
cell has no certification support and the model class contains observationally
indistinguishable safe and unsafe worlds, no validation-only protocol can both
uniformly control false acceptance and nontrivially accept in the safe world.

## Statistical Boundaries

The new primary paired comparison counts point-selection and vector-envelope
contract violations across the four supported datasets inside each seed, then
uses a two-sided exact sign test on nonzero seed differences. Safety uses one
prespecified rotating dataset decision per seed. Usefulness and deployment
uncertainty resample whole seed clusters with the locked bootstrap seed. The
overall claim requires every primary gate to pass, so it is an
intersection--union decision. Per-dataset secondary tests use Holm correction.
Candidate edits, attackers, Gamma values, budgets, and the four decisions
inside a seed are not independent replicates.

The outcome-blind supplementary safety sensitivity, SHA-256
`3c0676044ff126831d5ddbc9267fc5e4e8044df51385f77b31bd79a827553baf`,
reports the probability of any vector-rule violation among the four datasets
within a seed plus per-dataset exact bounds. It cannot replace a failed rotating
sentinel gate or be described as preregistered primary inference.

The registered vector/common gate uses the point ratio. Its original bootstrap
interval is reproduced unchanged, but the separately frozen sensitivity,
SHA-256 `b5325c8499ed493ae5c3469af27a9df17760b31a69dae8fb2beac2bc5582cec8`,
must also report every zero-common bootstrap sample and the division-free
`(V-2C)/O` interval. The sensitivity cannot turn a failed point-ratio gate into
a pass.

No unfavorable result may be removed. A failed primary pass condition cannot
be replaced by a secondary ablation, alternate threshold, alternate shift
budget, or exploratory seed result.

## Cross-Venue Result Boundary

AAAI-27, ICLR 2027, and NeurIPS 2027 are content variants of one scientific
record. Every controlled numerator, denominator, interval, adjusted value,
pass/fail statement, reason code, title branch, and negative result must derive
from the same audited `vera_shared_result_manifest.json` under
`SHARED_RESULT_MANIFEST_SPEC.md`. Venue-specific prose may shorten an
explanation but may not recompute, reround, relabel, or omit an unfavorable
result.

The ICLR and NeurIPS content variants may emphasize different aspects of the
same contribution. ICLR foregrounds representation interventions, retrained
attackers, ablations, and failure diagnosis. NeurIPS foregrounds the supported
probability model, simultaneous envelope, evidence allocation, exact-KL
Bernoulli lower bound, and impossibility theorem. Neither emphasis creates an
additional theorem, dataset, endpoint, or empirical claim.

The title **VERA: Evidence-Efficient Certification of Representation Edits
Under Deployment Shift** is conditional. It remains only if the frozen final
primary-source search leaves no unaddressed allocation collision and the
additive allocator beats both uniform and locked square-score allocation under
the registered usefulness rule without worse safety. Otherwise every venue
variant uses the frozen support-aware fallback title and reports the allocation
study as a negative or limited ablation.

Scientific-content completion does not establish official-format readiness or
authorize overlapping archival review. ICLR and NeurIPS remain format-pending
until their official 2027 materials and current duplicate-submission policies
are verified by a human author.

## Forbidden Claims

- Do not claim that VERA invents LTT, Prompt Risk Control, conformal risk
  control, finite-family testing, generic robust validation, or generic
  fairness certification.
- Do not claim universal concept erasure, security against unregistered
  attackers, privacy, fairness for unmeasured concepts, or eraser optimality.
- Do not claim target preservation for an unregistered downstream task or
  learner; paired-harm control is scoped to the target protocol in the receipt.
- Do not claim external-distribution validity without the stated support and
  density-ratio assumptions.
- Do not recode Camelyon17 support mismatch as a measured external violation.
- Do not claim clinical safety or clinical deployment evidence.
- Do not claim acceptance at AAAI, ICLR, ISEF, or any other venue is likely or
  guaranteed on the basis of internal audits.
- Do not hide, minimize, or remove the manuscript's generative-AI assistance
  disclosure.
