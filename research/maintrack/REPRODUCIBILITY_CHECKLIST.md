## Data And Methods

The real matrix contains five public datasets: Waterbirds,
Camelyon17-WILDS, CivilComments-WILDS, Bios, and GaitPDB. It crosses five
pinned official eraser implementations: INLP, R-LACE, LEACE, TaCo, and
MANCE++. One receipt is required for every dataset, eraser, and untouched seed,
for exactly 200 original balanced-confirmation official-code receipts and zero
proxy rows. The
independent stress replication repeats the official-code matrix on 32 disjoint
seeds, for 800 additional receipts under a separate preregistration. Each
receipt records the upstream remote and commit, runner commit, split hashes,
preregistration hash, candidate settings, and per-example NPZ hashes.

The controlled supported-shift study adds 1,280 receipts from four supported
datasets, five eraser families, and 64 fresh seeds. Each of its 3,072 candidate
archives includes identity and edited target errors, paired harm, four
registered attacker arrays, and one held-out boosted-tree stress array. Shift
receipts record the exact density-ratio profile, focus cell, allocation, and
independent final-stream hash.

No external fold constructs an edit or trains a target model or attacker. In
the prior IID studies, the external split is opened only for locked evaluation.
In the controlled-shift study, its metadata choose the supported focus cell and
its outcomes may determine only the prospective evidence allocation; neither
can read certification-stream or final-stream outcomes. Camelyon17 center 2 is
outside certification support and therefore forces VERA to abstain; its
single-class external source slice makes balanced leakage non-estimable.

## Statistical Checks

- The candidate-wise fixed-profile intersection-union test spends
  `delta / 12` per candidate and requires every component contract to pass.
- The post-selection envelope uses simultaneous coverage over all registered
  candidate-contract pairs.
- The exact balanced study contains 54 cells and 2,000 repetitions per cell.
- The family/grid extension contains 216 cells and 2,000 repetitions per cell.
- Primary real-data inference averages the nine correlated threshold outcomes
  within each seed and applies an exact one-sided sign-flip test, followed by
  Holm correction over the four externally estimable datasets.
- The independent stress replication uses one locked contract per supported
  dataset and requires, without post-hoc replacement, point-selection external
  violations at or above 20%, VERA violations at or below `delta`, and
  Holm-corrected one-sided paired McNemar `p <= .05` on all four externally
  estimable datasets. Camelyon17 remains the forced-abstention support-boundary
  case.
- A sign-only test is a secondary robustness check. Configuration-level
  McNemar and Clopper-Pearson calculations are reported as dependent-cell
  diagnostics, not independent-trial inference.
- Safe-retention uncertainty is reported with a seed-cluster bootstrap; the
  configuration-level binomial interval remains a labeled diagnostic.
- The controlled study's primary profile is `Gamma=1.1`, a 4,000-observation
  targeted allocation with a 15% floor per registered evidence cell.
- That locked primary uses the registered square-score allocator. The additive
  multi-cell extension uses a separate deterministic stream namespace and may
  not replace any primary gate.
- Its four primary gates are a seed-cluster exact sign test, a rotating-sentinel
  one-sided exact safety bound, a seed-cluster lower confidence bound on safe
  retention, and a vector/common retention ratio of at least two.
- The rotating sentinel probabilities may be heterogeneous. Its registered
  safety gate can pass only at zero events; the zero-event limit is conservative
  for their average risk by the AM--GM product bound. Nonzero-event binomial
  intervals are descriptive.
- A frozen supplementary sensitivity reports the seed-level probability of any
  vector-rule violation among the four datasets and per-dataset exact bounds.
  It cannot replace the rotating-sentinel primary gate.
- Primary violation labels and oracle opportunities use exact shifted
  finite-law expectations. The independent 50,000-draw stream is a Monte Carlo
  replay and cannot alter those labels.
- Secondary `Gamma` values, evidence budgets, allocations, candidates, and
  attackers remain correlated analyses and never replace a failed primary gate.

### Required Controlled-Shift Completion Order

The command list above names the locked analyzer but is not, by itself, the
final controlled-study reproduction. Before controlled results become
submission-facing, the final pipeline must execute and receipt the following
order:

1. verify all protocol and interpretation sidecars and apply
   `CONTROLLED_SHIFT_RECEIPT_AUDIT_INTERPRETATION_1.md` and its canonical-token
   clarification in `CONTROLLED_SHIFT_RECEIPT_AUDIT_INTERPRETATION_2.md`, requiring the exact
   1,280-receipt and 3,072-candidate file sets, preregistered path roots, closed
   NPZ members, and all corruption fixtures;
2. complete the official receipt audit with zero missing, invalid, proxy, dirty,
   or revision-mismatched rows;
3. implement and test the protocol-compliant cap-8 analyzer, code-path-
   independent cap-8 replay, unchanged cap-4 sensitivity runner, three-way
   comparator, safety/usefulness/ratio sensitivities, three-rule threshold
   stress, additive allocation, evidence diagnostic, theorem-to-code audit,
   shared-result builder, legacy result-macro retirement, Figure 1 rebuild,
   source flattening, presentation audit, cross-venue audit, and archive
   hardening using synthetic or source-only fixtures;
4. freeze, commit, and push that outcome-blind source state, then record its
   commit and every source/specification hash before generating an aggregate;
5. generate sealed outputs from the independent cap-8 replay, the protocol-
   compliant cap-8 analyzer, and the byte-unchanged cap-4 analyzer without
   printing or reading any scientific value; hash each sealed output;
6. make the three-way comparator the first permitted aggregate outcome read:
   require the two cap-8 paths to agree exactly within the frozen tolerance,
   retain cap 4 only as a sensitivity, report every cap-induced decision or
   gate difference, and record the access time, command, input hashes, and
   output hash in the outcome-access ledger;
7. run the registered safety, usefulness, vector/common, and zero-denominator
   sensitivities plus the three-rule, three-threshold stress without changing a
   primary gate;
8. run all 16 ablations, held-out attacker stress, and the GaitPDB diagnosis;
9. run the additive allocation comparison and inverse evidence-requirement
   diagnostic on their disjoint registered streams;
10. generate the one authoritative JSON result manifest, TeX result layer,
    main table, result figure, full supplement tables, and negative-result
    coverage report, then validate the JSON against the frozen Draft 2020-12
    schema; and
11. audit number, claim, gate, title, and limitation equality across flattened
    AAAI, ICLR, and NeurIPS sources and PDFs.

No later step may repair a failed earlier gate by changing thresholds, profiles,
seeds, rules, exclusions, or endpoint status.

archive additionally runs a compact frozen-row replay of those independent
selection decisions.

## Release Policy

Release source code, frozen preregistrations, compact aggregate rows, receipt
JSON files, audit reports, figures, paper sources, and the anonymous archive.
Do not commit third-party datasets, derived embedding stores, or private human
review/account records. Public-data derivatives remain on external storage and
are represented by manifests and content hashes.

`research/maintrack/DATA_SOFTWARE_LICENSE_LEDGER.md` is fail-closed. Upstream
repositories or data with absent, pending, or restrictive terms are not
redistributed. Final packaging requires official-source verification of every
row and any required notices. Double-Blind Interpretation 3, SHA-256
`df58d132c19f26ce37b7a3c4eccc1b79da260214c62eddcf97c86cdc79e0d754`,
also requires a human-approved license for VERA-authored material, an exact
archive-copy hash, and an explicit third-party exclusion list. No automation
may infer that approval or extend the VERA grant to external material.

## Final Human Gates

Technical audits do not establish novelty, proof correctness, ethical
eligibility, or likely acceptance. Before submission, the human author must
verify every official checklist response, confirm the venue deadline and
OpenReview account, inspect the anonymous archive, and close four role-specific
cold reviews plus one fresh post-revision review from researchers who publish
in machine learning. The manuscript's
generative-AI disclosure must remain present and must accurately describe the
full scope of assistance.
