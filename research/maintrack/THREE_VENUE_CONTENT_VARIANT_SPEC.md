# Three-Venue Scientific Content Specification

This specification was fixed before any seeds 45--108 scientific outcome was
viewed. It defines what it means for the AAAI-27, ICLR 2027, and NeurIPS 2027
content variants to be scientifically complete. It does not authorize
simultaneous archival submission and does not invent unavailable venue rules.

## One Scientific Record

All variants must derive empirical values from the same audited result macros,
theory from `appendix_shift_robust_theory.tex`, references from
`references_verified.bib`, and claim boundaries from `CLAIM_LEDGER.md`.
They must agree on:

1. datasets, methods, candidates, seeds, thresholds, attackers, and shifts;
2. preregistered primary and secondary endpoints;
3. every numerator, denominator, interval, adjusted value, and gate result;
4. the prior IID composite miss and every new negative controlled result;
5. the finite-reference and registered-attacker scope of the guarantee;
6. the Camelyon17 unsupported-support interpretation;
7. the distinction between the locked square-score primary and the disjoint
   additive-allocation extension; and
8. the complete three-rule, three-threshold supporting stress and its
   non-rescuing readiness decision; and
9. the generative-AI assistance disclosure and human-responsibility boundary.

No venue variant may omit an unfavorable primary result, substitute a secondary
profile for a failed primary gate, alter a threshold, or strengthen a claim
because another venue permits more pages. Any venue-specific shortened result
must point to a complete table in its own permitted supplement, without making
that supplement necessary to understand the central claim.

## AAAI-27 Variant

The AAAI variant is the compact general-AI decision-layer paper. Its seven
technical pages prioritize:

- the representation-edit deployment question;
- the exact distinction from Learn Then Test and generic robust validation;
- the vector envelope, common radius, and support boundary;
- the controlled Gamma-greater-than-one deployment-rule comparison; and
- the always-deploy versus validation-selection versus VERA threshold-stress
  result at all three fixed severities; and
- one main result table plus one teaching figure and one three-panel result
  figure, subject to actual page fit.

The official AAAI-27 author kit is available. Readiness requires the single-TeX
finalization, exact page/font checks, clean anonymous package, separate
checklist, and every human gate in the venue plan.

## ICLR 2027 Content Variant

The ICLR content variant emphasizes representation learning and intervention
evaluation:

- why an eraser output is an intervention rather than a complete solution;
- paired target effects and recovery by heterogeneous retrained attackers;
- the geometry and practical advantage of anisotropic edit-shift envelopes;
- component ablations across eraser families, attacker classes, and candidate
  frontiers; and
- evidence growth, limiting cells, and the GaitPDB diagnosis.

It must contain a complete abstract, introduction, related work, setup, method,
guarantees, protocol, results, ablations, limitations, responsible-use section,
references, and supplement plan. Until official ICLR 2027 materials exist and
are verified, it is labeled `SCIENTIFIC CONTENT COMPLETE, FORMAT PENDING`, not
submission ready. The legacy ICLR 2026 files are not an official 2027 format.

## NeurIPS 2027 Content Variant

The NeurIPS content variant emphasizes statistical decision theory and
evidence design:

- the supported conditional reweighting model and vector confidence envelope;
- fixed-profile IUT validity versus simultaneous post-inspection validity;
- exact discrete curves and continuum inversion;
- additive multi-cell minimax evidence allocation, its special cases, and the
  exact-KL lower bound; and
- controlled safety, power, allocation, and sample-size experiments.

It must contain the same complete scientific sections and empirical record as
the other variants. Until official NeurIPS 2027 materials exist and are
verified, it is labeled `SCIENTIFIC CONTENT COMPLETE, FORMAT PENDING`, not
submission ready.

## Shared Result Insertion

One generated, audited macro source must supply every headline result to all
three variants. A cross-variant audit must fail on:

- a number present in prose but absent from the audited result manifest;
- different values or confidence statements for the same endpoint;
- different pass/fail descriptions of a registered gate;
- a missing required negative-result sentence;
- a threshold-stress headline taken from a severity other than the registered
  `kappa=1` contract, or an omitted stress cell;
- title use of `Evidence-Efficient` when the frozen title-retention rule fails;
  or
- a venue variant that calls format-pending material submission ready.

## Packaging and Submission Boundary

Named author copies may be retained privately and in the named development
repository where policy permits. Anonymous review PDFs, flattened upload
sources, and review archives follow `DOUBLE_BLIND_RELEASE_BOUNDARY.md` and must
not be committed to the named branch.

AAAI, ICLR, and NeurIPS are archival targets. The same or substantially similar
work must not be under overlapping review when official duplicate-submission
rules forbid it. Human authors must verify the current rules and choose a legal
sequence of submission, withdrawal, decision, and revision.

## Pass Condition

This specification passes only when:

1. all three full scientific-content variants exist;
2. a cross-variant claim-and-number audit passes;
3. each uses the final authoritative theory and bibliography;
4. all figures, tables, negative results, limitations, and disclosures are
   complete;
5. AAAI passes its available official-format checks;
6. ICLR and NeurIPS pass official-format checks once their 2027 materials are
   available; and
7. human authors confirm duplicate-submission, anonymity, authorship, and AI
   policies before each actual submission.
