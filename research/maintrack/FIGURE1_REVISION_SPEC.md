# Figure 1 Revision Specification

This outcome-blind specification freezes the final teaching goal for Figure 1.
It changes presentation only. It does not change a candidate, threshold,
allocation rule, endpoint, or result.

## Ten-Second Message

VERA uses an independent design fold to put limited certification evidence where
the certificate is expected to be tight, then deploys an edit only inside the
simultaneously certified, supported shift envelope.

## Panel A: Allocate, Then Audit

- Title: `A Allocate, then audit the edit`.
- Show three or four abstract evidence cells with design-fold scores `A_j` and a
  compact arrow to counts `n_j proportional to A_j`, with the registered floor
  indicated by a thin baseline rather than a paragraph.
- Show that one shared source-class draw evaluates all registered attackers; do
  not draw attacker-specific samples as independent evidence.
- Retain one short paired identity-versus-edit row illustrating
  `H_e in {-1,0,1}` and four fresh attacker icons or labeled circles.
- Remove the duplicate `paired target harm` label and keep every attacker label
  directly under its marker with no collisions.
- Use the caption to state that scores come from an independent design fold and
  certification outcomes remain untouched.

## Panel B: Fixed Profile and Envelope

- Title: `B Test one profile; report the envelope`.
- Keep threshold-normalized component upper curves, the fixed declared profile,
  and the limiting common radius.
- Use a light fill for the all-contract feasible region and label only the
  limiting curve in the plot; move the full attacker key to a compact legend.
- Distinguish the candidate IUT from the simultaneous envelope in the panel or
  caption: the first decides the registered profile, while the second supports
  post-certification inspection over a continuum.
- Curves remain explicitly schematic and must not resemble empirical outcomes.

## Panel C: New Support Forces Abstention

- Title: `C New support means abstain`.
- Keep the Camelyon17 support example: certification centers `0, 1, 3, 4` versus
  unseen center `2`.
- Replace `Radius CERTIFIED` with `Supported profile can be certified` so the
  box does not imply that the displayed dataset received a positive radius.
- Keep `New support: ABSTAIN` and the one-class warning.
- State that this is an identification boundary, not evidence of clinical harm.

## Visual Constraints

- Exactly three panels in one full-width vector figure.
- Colorblind-safe palette with line style and marker redundancy.
- Minimum effective text size of 7 pt in the AAAI two-column build.
- No text overlap, clipped labels, rasterized type, or unexplained acronym.
- Readable at 50% PDF zoom and in a grayscale print.
- Alt-text equivalent must be preserved in the caption or supplement.

## Acceptance Checks

1. A cold reader can answer what is allocated, what is certified, and why VERA
   abstains without reading the method section.
2. The evidence-allocation step appears visually because it is part of the title
   and second contribution.
3. Panel A has no label collisions at the final page size.
4. Panel B does not imply that finite-family testing itself is novel.
5. Panel C does not imply that Camelyon17 establishes a clinical failure.
6. The final PDF passes vector-font, grayscale, 50%-zoom, and visual-overlap
   inspection in both named and anonymous builds.
