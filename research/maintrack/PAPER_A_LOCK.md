# Paper A Lock

Paper A is the VERA method paper. Science-fair framing is out of scope.

## Locked Thesis

VERA converts representation erasure from an IID edit-selection problem into a
shift-scoped certification problem. For every registered edit, it reports a
simultaneous lower confidence bound on the largest bounded density-ratio shift
under which paired target harm and every registered post-edit leakage contract
remain valid. It returns `ABSTAIN` when the IID contract cannot be established
or when deployment includes support absent from certification.

## Contribution Sentence

VERA is a shift-aware decision layer over representation interventions. INLP,
R-LACE, LEACE, TaCo, and MANCE++ construct candidate edits; VERA lower-certifies
their groupwise paired-harm and heterogeneous-attacker **erasure shift
envelope**, with the common shift radius as its minimum coordinate.
The finite-family testing mechanism is attributed to Learn Then Test and Pareto
Testing, while the proposed contribution is the erasure-specific robust object,
its support boundary, and the evidence that the distinction changes deployment
decisions.

## Evidence Lock

The locked evidence package consists of a 54-cell exact balanced study, a
216-cell exact candidate/group-count extension, and a preregistered 200-run
official-code matrix: five erasers, eight untouched seeds (5--12), and five
datasets spanning vision, medical vision, text, and time series. Every real run
must have a compact receipt tied to one Git commit, one preregistration hash,
one pinned upstream commit, and hashes for every external audit array. External
outcomes are unused by construction and selection, and aggregate external
analysis is deferred until the entire matrix and receipt audit complete.
